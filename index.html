Bio

Dr. Laurie Hoke has devoted her career to the health and well being of school age children; she has worked tirelessly to support their social, emotional and academic needs. With over 23 years experience as a practicing School Psychologist and 5 years as a Special Education Administrator, she has extensive knowledge of children’s behavioral, social, emotional, and educational development.

Dr. Hoke holds a Doctorate in Educational Psychology, and a Master of Education in School Psychology, both earned at Loyola University in Chicago, Illinois. Her Bachelor of Arts in School Psychology was earned at Michigan State University.

Throughout her tenure working in education, Dr. Hoke has interacted with students and their families to ensure that they have a full understanding of the total process involved with securing the best possible support for each individual child. This includes the assessment process, what to expect at school meetings, and your rights as parents.

Dr. Hoke has been a resource for families nation-wide. For years, parents have sought her guidance and expertise when they are dealing with difficult issues regarding their children’s needs at school. Whether it’s a support the child is not getting, or the school is requesting a special meeting, she has been an invaluable asset for parents. When students with severe disabilities require additional services and/or placement, she provides parents with clear, distinct information that will assist them in navigating this process.

Dr. Hoke strives to advise and coach parents so when they attend school meetings, they are prepared with the appropriate questions, and knowledge of their child’s entitlements. Her expertise in the facilitation of such school meetings over many years makes her an excellent resource for parents.
