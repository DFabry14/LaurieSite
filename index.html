Laurie Hoke, Ed.D

Educational Concierge

(847) 778-9415 · LEHoke16@gmail.com

Dr. Laurie Hoke has devoted her career to the health and well-being of school age children, working tirelessly to support their social emotional and academic health and advise their families on ways to support their needs. With a Doctorate Degree in Educational Psychology and a Master’s Degree in school psychology, both from Loyola University of Chicago Dr. Hoke has vast knowledge and experience with children’s behavior, social, emotional and educational development.

Dr. Hoke has over 23 years as a school psychologist and 5 years as a special education administrator. Throughout her tenure working in education, Dr. Hoke has worked with students and their families to ensure that families have a full understanding of the steps to take to obtain the necessary supports for their children, the assessment process and what takes place at meetings for their children.

Dr. Hoke has been a resource for families nation-wide. Parents seek out Dr. Hoke to provide guidance when there is a support their child is not getting, when the school contacts parents to invite them in for meetings and when parents of students with severe disabilities require additional supports or placements throughout their education. Dr. Hoke provides parents with clear, distinct information that will assist them in navigating the process of obtaining supports for their children in school as well as understanding their rights in working with the school when a need is identified by the teacher.

Dr. Hoke strives to educate and coach parents so that they attend meetings prepared and knowing what they are entitled to, as well as what questions to ask. Her expertise in the facilitation of such meetings in the school setting makes her an excellent resource for families as they prepare to deal with the schools’ administration on their child’s behalf. Laurie’s insights into this process afford parents the important ability to educate themselves before attending a meeting where decisions may be made regarding the education of their child. This ability enhances the success of parents working in conjunction with the school system to advocate for the supports their child needs.
